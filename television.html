OLED vs QLED TVs

OLED TVs

OLED (Organic Light Emitting Diodes) TVs offer a superior image quality thanks to their ability to display true blacks. Each pixel in an OLED display is individually lit, meaning it can be turned off completely to produce a true black. This results in an infinite contrast ratio, and allows for a very thin screen.

Pros of OLED TVs

  • Superior image quality with true blacks
  • Fast refresh rate, ideal for sports and fast action movies
  • Thin and lightweight design

Cons of OLED TVs

  • Can be more expensive than other types of TVs
  • Longevity could be less than LED TVs
  • Screen burn-in could be a potential issue with static images

QLED TVs

QLED (Quantum Dot LED) TVs are a variation of LED LCD TVs â€” they use a quantum dot filter that increases the color range and brightness. QLED TVs can't produce true blacks like OLEDs, but they can get brighter, which makes them a better choice for a bright room.

Pros of QLED TVs

  • Brighter than OLED TVs, making them a good choice for bright rooms
  • Long lifespan
  • Less risk of burn-in compared to OLED

Cons of QLED TVs

  • Can't produce true blacks like OLED TVs
  • Viewing angles are not as wide as OLED TVs
  • Heavier and thicker than OLED TVs
