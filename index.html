A Person for All of Wisconsin

Are you tired of politicians who seem more interested in fighting each other than fighting for you? I am. I’m not a career politician. I’m a neighbor, a student, a friend to many, and a wisconsinite who has seen the promise of Wisconsin get buried under partisan gridlock.

I’m running for Governor to change that. My pledge is simple: to be a leader for everyone, regardless of their zip code or who they voted for in the last election. This campaign is about replacing division with dialogue, restoring common sense, and ensuring your voice is heard in Wisconsin. It’s time for a government that works for the people again.

My Platform for Wisconsin

Practical policies for a prosperous and united state.

Economic Growth & Job Creation

Championing small businesses, investing in modern manufacturing and development, and cutting red tape to make Wisconsin the best place to work and build a future.

Affordable Healthcare

Working to lower prescription drug costs for all wisconsinites, protect rural hospitals, and ensure every Wisconsinite has access to quality, and affordable healthcare.

Education Excellence

Investing in our public schools, supporting our teachers and students, and expanding access to technical education to prepare our kids for the jobs of today.

Diving Deeper: My Policy Priorities

Click on an issue to see my detailed approach.

My plan focuses on targeted tax relief for small businesses, not giant corporations. We will invest in apprenticeship programs in high-demand fields like advanced manufacturing and renewable energy, and work to bring high-speed internet to every corner of the state to unlock economic potential for all.

We will pursue policies that decrease healthcare costs for all, invest in mental health services, and expand access to preventative care and telehealth services, especially in rural communities. Your health should not depend on your zip code.

My administration will work to increase teacher pay to attract and retain the best educators. We will fully fund our public schools, and partner with technical colleges to ensure our graduates are prepared for the jobs of today
